software vulnerability Report 01

Decade-Old phpBB Flaw Fixed

The phpBB development team released security updates addressing a critical authentication bypass vulnerability that had remained undiscovered for nearly a decade. The flaw could allow attackers to improperly authenticate as other users under specific conditions, potentially leading to unauthorized account access and privilege abuse. Researchers noted that the vulnerability affected multiple phpBB versions and persisted through numerous software releases before being identified and corrected. Because phpBB remains widely deployed across community forums and online discussion platforms, the issue carried significant security implications. Administrators are urged to update immediately, review account activity, and verify that forum deployments are running the latest supported versions.

phishing scam Report 02

Fake Facebook Offers Fuel Scams

Cybersecurity researchers uncovered widespread scam campaigns targeting users across the Middle East and North Africa through fraudulent Facebook accounts, fake promotional offers, and deceptive browser alerts. The attackers impersonate politicians, public figures, government organizations, and well-known brands to build credibility and lure victims into revealing credentials and personal information. Analysts observed social engineering techniques designed to create urgency, including fake prize notifications, account warnings, and security alerts. The campaigns primarily focus on credential theft and account compromise, enabling further fraud and identity abuse. Researchers advise users to verify sources, avoid suspicious links, and enable multifactor authentication for online accounts.

network vulnerability Report 03

Palo Alto Flaw Under Active Attack

Palo Alto Networks warned that attackers are actively exploiting CVE-2026-0257, an authentication bypass vulnerability affecting PAN-OS GlobalProtect portals and gateways. The flaw enables unauthorized users to establish VPN connections without valid credentials under specific configurations involving authentication override cookies and certificate settings. Security researchers observed successful exploitation attempts beginning in mid-May, with attacks targeting unpatched internet-facing devices. The vulnerability has been added to CISA’s Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog due to confirmed real-world abuse. Organizations are strongly advised to apply available patches, implement vendor-recommended mitigations, review VPN access logs, and monitor for suspicious authentication activity across affected environments.
