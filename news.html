INTERPOL Dismantles Major Phishing Platform

INTERPOL and regional law enforcement agencies dismantled SniperDz, one of the world's longest-running phishing-as-a-service platforms, during Operation Ramz. The coordinated operation spanned 13 Middle East and North Africa countries and resulted in 201 arrests, 53 server seizures, and identification of 382 suspects and 3,867 victims. Investigators arrested the alleged developer in Algeria and confiscated infrastructure used to support large-scale credential theft campaigns. SniperDz had enabled cybercriminals to launch phishing attacks for nearly a decade using ready-made tools and templates. Security experts view the takedown as a significant victory against organized cybercrime and phishing-as-a-service ecosystems.

AI security Report 02

AgentJacking Turns AI Into Attackers

Researchers disclosed a new attack technique called AgentJacking that targets AI-powered coding assistants and autonomous development agents. The attack abuses trusted error-monitoring platforms by injecting malicious instructions into application error reports that appear indistinguishable from legitimate remediation guidance. When AI coding agents retrieve and analyze these reports, they can unknowingly execute attacker-controlled commands on developer workstations. Security analysts warn that the technique effectively transforms AI assistants into privileged execution tools capable of running arbitrary code, accessing sensitive files, and modifying development environments. The findings highlight growing risks associated with autonomous coding agents and reinforce the need for strict validation, sandboxing, and human oversight.

security strategy Report 03

MDR Evolves For AI-Powered Threats

As attackers increasingly leverage AI to accelerate phishing, reconnaissance, and exploitation, organizations are rethinking the role of Managed Detection and Response services. Modern MDR platforms are evolving beyond alert monitoring to include proactive threat prevention, exposure management, automated investigations, identity protection, and coordinated response capabilities. Industry reports show a growing demand for AI-assisted security operations as defenders struggle with alert fatigue, staffing shortages, and machine-speed attacks. Security experts emphasize that successful MDR programs must combine human expertise, automation, threat intelligence, and continuous validation to reduce risk. The shift reflects a broader transition from reactive security operations toward prevention-first cyber resilience strategies.
